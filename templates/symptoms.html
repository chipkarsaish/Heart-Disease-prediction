Symptoms of Heart Disease

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

A buildup of plaque in the arteries can reduce blood flow to the heart.

  • Chest pain, tightness, or pressure (Angina).
  • Shortness of breath.
  • Pain or numbness in the limbs due to reduced blood flow.
  • Pain in the neck, jaw, throat, or back.

Arrhythmias (Irregular Heartbeat)

When the heartbeat is too fast, too slow, or irregular.

  • Dizziness or fainting.
  • Rapid, slow, or skipped heartbeats.
  • Fluttering sensation in the chest.

Causes of Heart Disease

High Blood Pressure

Uncontrolled blood pressure can weaken heart muscles and arteries.

Poor Diet & Obesity

Excessive consumption of cholesterol, salt, and sugar increases the risk.

Smoking & Alcohol

Nicotine and alcohol contribute to arterial damage and heart strain.
